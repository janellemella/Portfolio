February and March are the months when folks start scrambling to make last-minute decisions about where to go for spring break. For high school and college students, it's especially tricky, since many travel without parental guidance and often have limited budgets, so every choice matters. The most important factor that can make or break a trip? The location. There's a lot to consider: will the weather be sunny and warm enough for a tan, or will the week feel slow if there aren't beaches or hiking trails to explore? Some travelers chase nonstop partying at a luxurious resort, others prefer chill sunsets and relaxation and some want a mix of it all.

Picking the right spot can be difficult. According to the American Automobile Association, in 2025 about 60% of Americans traveled domestically for spring break, meaning many young travelers are likely to be staying in the U.S. this year as well. AAA also notes that spring break is often the “first big vacation of the year” for many, so it's a trip that can set the tone for the months ahead.

Luckily, there's a solution. This Spring Break Quiz can help take away the uncertainty about where to go and what to do, pointing each traveler toward a destination that fits their vacation needs perfectly.
